<h2 id="framework">Our Frameworks</h2>

<section class="framework-section">
  <h3 id="conference-framework">Conference</h3>
  <p>
    Security2Cure events follow a deliberate structure designed to balance technical depth with
    meaningful human connection. Each conference day is built around four pillars:
  </p>
  <ol class="framework-list">
    <li><strong>Technical Excellence:</strong> Cutting-edge cyber security content across offensive, defensive, and governance domains.</li>
    <li><strong>Personal Narratives:</strong> Industry colleagues sharing how cancer has impacted their lives or the lives of loved ones.</li>
    <li><strong>Medical Education:</strong> Qualified health professionals providing evidence-based prevention and detection guidance.</li>
    <li><strong>Community Connection:</strong> Structured networking, sponsor engagement, and opportunities to support the charity mission.</li>
  </ol>
</section>

<section class="framework-section framework-section--health">
  <h3 id="health-framework">Health</h3>
  <p>
    Alongside the conference programme, Security2Cure promotes a practical approach to personal
    health that speaks the language of cyber security. Many of us plan for incidents at work, yet
    fewer of us apply the same discipline to our own wellbeing, screening, and family preparedness.
  </p>
  <p>
    Our health framework maps familiar security concepts to everyday health decisions: planning before
    a crisis, preventing avoidable harm, detecting problems early, and responding with support when
    it matters most. It is not medical advice, but a way to start conversations, reduce stigma, and
    help our community look after themselves and each other.
  </p>
</section>
